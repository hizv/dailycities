To embark onto 2023, we decided to celebrate the music of 2022. We each were given 30 minutes to write a piece about one of our favorite releases of the year. This is the result of that collaboration.

  • The Soft Pink Truth - Is It Going to Get Any Deeper Than This?

    2022, Thrill Jockey

    by Booshnaw

    Under the The Soft Pink Truth moniker, Drew Daniel has creatively subverted dance music expectations by tackling very interesting and sometimes contradictory concepts. Each of his LPs asks a question in it's namesake. For example, taking a super minimalistic electroclash approach to Hardcore/Anarcho Punk staples on 2004's *Do You Want New Wave or Do You Want The Soft Pink Truth*? (e.t. *Deeper Than This*?). On 2022's *Is It Going to Get Any Deeper Than This?*, The Soft Pink Truth has come upon probably the most interesting of these prompts. I love the story behind this question: it being directed at Daniel and a friend from someone visiting their DJ set. The core idea of *Deeper Than This?* is the of idea of trying to answer the question in a variety of musical forms, all centered around *deepness*.

    From beginning to end, this dwelling on deepness is the primary musical backdrop -- extremely pure and moody dance tracks through and through. It may seem like redundant praise, but I do really enjoy how many aspects of this record tackle "deepness". There is depth in the amount of styles "deepified", not just staying in the traditional Deep House lane. Many styles are incorporated into the depths, embracing Dance music with layered, intricate, and moody production. On "La Joie Devant La Mort" there is deep French Pop set into an glamorous Nu-Disco track. "Sunwash" is probably the deepest of the bunch, swirling synths and creeping production changes make for a hypnotic ambient Deep House experience.

    I could go into much more depth on this given a different written format, but at the end of the day I think the most important question to answer is in the album title. I'd say it does get deeper than *this*, however Dance music in general can only go so deep. In fact I'd say the mission of this record was an amazing success. It's mission isn't to prove how deep one could go, but is instead a creative retort to a strange question. The layers of deepness, from the question itself to how the concept of deepeness is applied to the music in many fascinating ways makes for an incredible record. Like damn y'all, this is some beautiful and crisp Deep House.

    In terms of why I picked this particular album to write on, I'd say the answer is two-fold. First, I've already extensively written on Chat Pile's *God's Country* on this very site (check it out!). Secondly and most important, this record just hits it out of the park in ways no other did this year. From the initial moment I heard the prelude I was hooked. I am continually stunned by the depth presented in concept, abstract, and execution. There is nothing like this, just enjoy the Deep House ride into the depths.
